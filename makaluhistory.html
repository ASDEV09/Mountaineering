Makalu

Makalu, standing proudly as the fifth highest peak globally, is a formidable giant nestled in the Himalayan range, straddling the border between Nepal and Tibet (China). This majestic mountain, with an elevation of 8,485 meters (27,838 feet), has a storied history that echoes the relentless spirit of exploration and mountaineering triumphs

The successful climb took place on May 15, 1955, led by a French expedition under the leadership of Jean Franco. The climbers who reached the summit were Lionel Terray and Jean Couzy. The expedition chose the challenging route up the north face and northeast ridge of Makalu, a demanding ascent characterized by technical difficulties, steep faces, and high-altitude challenges. Terray and Couzy's achievement marked not only the first ascent of Makalu but also demonstrated the mountaineering prowess required to conquer one of the world's most formidable peaks. This successful climb opened the door for subsequent expeditions and cemented Makalu's reputation as a challenging and sought-after destination for high-altitude climbers.

Arun River is in the east of the park and Sagarmatha National Park is in the west. It is a famous wildlife sanctuary, plants of all major ecological zones: tropical, sub-tropical, temperate, alpine and sub-alpine are found here. Rare species of animals and plants flourish in diverse climates. Over 3000 species of flowering plants, 440 species of birds, and 75 species of mammals, including endangered snow leopard, red panda and musk deer, have been recorded.

The Makalu Barun National Park and Conservation Area, established in 1992 to protect its unique biodiversity, require climbers to obtain a climbing permit and a National Park Entry Permit. Stringent environmental regulations aim to minimize the impact on fragile ecosystems, necessitating adherence to ethical practices. Local regulations control climber numbers, manage waste, ensuring safety, and preserving the pristine environment. Given the potential for evolving regulations, climbers and trekkers must stay informed about the latest guidelines before embarking on a Makalu expedition.

Most of the Makalu-Barun National Park is a remote wildness and is preferred by the professional mountaineers and biologists only. The abundant monsoon rains in the eastern Himalayan region supports diverse bio-climatic zones and have rich fauna and flora. Alpine pastures above 4000 m chiefly comprises of Dwarf Rhododendrons and Junipers. The Makalu Barun National Park also has various aromatic herbs and delicate wildflowers, including 47 different varieties of orchids. Some of the common tress includes sub-alpine Fir, Birch and Rhododendron. On higher altitude, the most common trees that can be found are Oak, Maple and Magnolia. On the lower altitudes Orchids Drape, Chestnut and Sal are found in abundance.
